# Platform Rules

Last updated: 2026-04-06
Status: active

## Purpose

Define the main product-facing differences between supported output platforms.

## YouTube Long

### Product use
Primary deep analytical video.

### Product emphasis
- coherent long-form narrative;
- block continuity;
- stronger explanatory density;
- packaging through title, thumbnail, and description.

## YouTube Shorts

### Product use
Short entry point into a deeper idea.

### Product emphasis
- one core mechanism or one sharp point;
- faster hook;
- vertical format;
- high clarity in the first moments.

## Instagram Reels

### Product use
Short emotionally legible or insight-driven film content.

### Product emphasis
- fast opening;
- strong emotional or conceptual point;
- vertical readability;
- caption and overlay text suitability.

## Instagram Carousel

### Product use
Swipeable analytical explanation built from condensed semantic blocks.

### Product emphasis
- clear slide progression;
- short readable text per slide;
- strong opening frame;
- clear final takeaway.

## Telegram or later text-support channels

### Product use
Supportive packaging and text distribution.

### Product emphasis
- clean summary;
- channel-style copy;
- bridge into long or short content.

## Product rule

The same project should support multiple platforms, but each platform must remain a specific view over the same semantic source rather than a disconnected rewrite system.
